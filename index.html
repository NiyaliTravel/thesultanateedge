The Island & The Atoll

Hinnavaru, Lhaviyani

Hinnavaru is one of Lhaviyani Atoll's principal islands — a genuine, busy Maldivian community surrounded by an atoll famous among divers.

Lhaviyani — known traditionally as Faadhippolhu — sits in the country's north, about 120 km from Malé. It is one of the Maldives' great diving atolls: fast channel drifts, famous wrecks at the Shipyard, turtle-rich reefs and lagoons that fade from jade to deep blue. Only four of its islands are inhabited, so the sea here still feels wide open.

Hinnavaru

One of the atoll's most populous islands and our home — a working harbour, coral-stone lanes, cafés and a dedicated visitors' beach.

Naifaru

The atoll capital, a short dhoni hop away — famous for its traditional medicine men and sea-turtle rescue work.

Kurendhoo & Olhuvelifushi

Lhaviyani's two other inhabited islands — quiet communities on the atoll's southern rim.

The dive sites

Kuredu Express, the Shipyard wrecks, Fushivaru Thila — channel drifts and cleaning stations that draw divers from around the world.
